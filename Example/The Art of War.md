#Book

## Planning

<card>
<fixed>
The five advantages of war are
</fixed>
<back>
The Moral Law, Heaven, Earth, The Commander, Method and Discipline.
</back>
</card>

Moral law causes the people to listen to the commander. Heaven signifies time of day and environmental factors that come into play in war, particularly temperature. Earth signifies the distance, narrowness and curvature of the terrain under mission; these play particularly important roles for the safety of the troop. The Commander signifies wisdom, sincerity, benevolence, courage and strictness. Method and Discipline refer to proper subdividing of troops, deliberation in officer promotion, maintaining supply lines (roads and infrastructure), and accounting of the army expenditures.

<card>
<fixed>
The five disadvantages of war are
</fixed>
<back>
recklessness, cowardice, a hasty temper provoked by insults, ego, over-solicitude for his men.
</back>
</card>

<card>
<fixed>
Wars of similar ability are won by
</fixed>
<back>
deception, preparedness and surprise.
</back>
</card>

Use baits to entice the enemy, feign disorder and crush him. If the enemy is secure, wait for him to make a move. Attack the enemy where he does not expect you, as he will be less secure.

<card>
<fixed>
Wars of uneven ability are won by
</fixed>
<back>
evading. Your best survival strategy is to not attack directly, but rather attack his systems. Separate his forces.
</back>
</card>

<card>
<fixed>
Long warfare should be
</fixed>
<back>
avoided at all costs. No country has benefited from prolonged warfare.
</back>
</card>

<card>
<fixed>
Use a conquered foe to
</fixed>
<back>
augment one's own strength.
</back>
</card>

Captured chariots should be primarily awarded to those that capture them (to motivate the soldiers), and captured soldiers should be treated like our own and fed and used in our own battles. For this reason we should always aim to capture the enemy intact, instead of destroying. The supreme art of war therefore is to avoid fight as it drains resources. The general should 1) prevent the enemys plans from being realized, 2) prevent enemies from joining forces, 3) attack the army in the field, and if all else fails 4) besiege walled cities.

<card>
<fixed>
The five essential understandings for victory are
</fixed>
<back>
when to fight, size, motivation, preparedness and diplomacy.
</back>
</card>

Know when to fight and not to fight.
Understand fighting with a smaller and with a larger army. 10:1 surround him, 2:1 attack on two fronts, <1:1 we should avoid contact, <<1:1 we must flee.
Motivate the army through every rank.
Be prepared and await an opening in the enemy's preparedness.
Avoid diplomacy in military capacity.

<card>
<fixed>
War must be only be sought if
</fixed>
<back>
victory can be assured.
</back>
</card>

A successful general waits prepared until his enemy shows weakness, then swiftly makes the enemy surrender. Fighting for "honor" is the path of defeat.

Weakness is typically based on haste. We can fool the enemy into hasting by providing them an advantage, making them rush to the battlefield only to arrive exhausted. We can also arrive at opposite sides of the enemy, making him rush to protect either side.

## Terrain

<card>
<fixed>

</fixed>
<back>
valleys, at small peaks that are protected from the environment and observation. Make sure the ridges on either side are not enemy territory, and the sun is in your enemies eyes. Avoid wet lands as it spreads diseases rapidly.
</back>
</card>

Valleys allow natural barricading and routing of supplies.

## Spies

<card>
<fixed>
The five classes of spies are
</fixed>
<back>
local (citizens), inward (officers), converted (enemy spies), doomed (sent into enemy territory with false information), and surviving (come back from enemy with intel).
</back>
</card>

The term spy in The Art of War refers to a generic intelligence agent, working to manipulate the flow of information. Our spies should target the second in line (friends and family) of our intelligence target, usually the enemy generals.
If an enemy spy is discovered, they must be treated hospitably. They will be our source of inward spies. The success of all other spies depend on the converted spies.
